Business Analyst Idea Notes
Context: Initial requirements gathering for a cultural project intake and evaluation platform

1. Perceived business goals
- Reduce time between submission and final result.
- Increase transparency and traceability of the selection process.
- Standardize evaluation criteria across different calls for proposals.
- Improve communication with applicants and reduce operational rework.

2. Mapped stakeholders (high level)
- Applicants (individuals/legal entities submitting projects).
- Internal review committee.
- External reviewers.
- Cultural program managers.
- Administrative/legal team.

3. Suggested MVP scope hypotheses
- Call for proposals creation and publication.
- Submission form with required documents.
- Project status workflow (submitted, under screening, under evaluation, approved/rejected).
- Configurable evaluation criteria and weights per call.
- Score entry by reviewers.
- Management dashboard for process tracking.
- Automatic email communication at key milestones.

4. Open questions for next refinement rounds
- Exact eligibility rules by event type.
- How to handle projects with missing documents (correction window, retry limits).
- Tie-break model for projects with identical scores.
- Whether appeals should be included in MVP or phase two.
- Accessibility requirements and plain-language form expectations.

5. Success criteria (initial proposal)
- 100% of submissions centralized in the platform (no parallel email process).
- At least 30% reduction in document screening time.
- Result publication with auditable project history.
- Positive baseline satisfaction from reviewers and managers regarding usability.

6. Perceived business risks
- Change resistance from teams used to spreadsheets.
- Call deadlines may pressure adoption without enough training.
- Poorly defined criteria may increase formal disputes and legal uncertainty.
- Scope expansion too early (risk of delaying core delivery).

7. Suggested approach
- Phase 1: end-to-end essential flow for one call.
- Phase 2: appeals, integrations, and advanced reporting.
- Conduct short workshops with reviewers to validate criteria and user experience.
