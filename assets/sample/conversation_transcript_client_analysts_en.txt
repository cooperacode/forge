Initial Requirements Meeting Transcript
Project: Cultural Event Project Intake and Evaluation Platform
Date: 2026-05-03
Participants:
- Client (Marina)
- Business Analyst (Rafael)
- Systems Analyst (Camila)

Marina (Client): Thank you for your time. The goal is to create a platform to receive proposals for cultural events, such as concerts, theater, films, musicals, and local festivals. Today we handle everything by email and spreadsheets, and it is becoming unmanageable.

Rafael (Business Analyst): Perfect. To understand the main objective: do you want to improve intake, evaluation, or both with equal priority?

Marina: Both. We need to publish calls for proposals, receive submissions, validate documents, score projects, and provide feedback to applicants.

Camila (Systems Analyst): When you mention document validation, do you expect automatic checks for file format and required fields only, or also content rules?

Marina: Initially, format and required items. In the future, maybe content checks too.

Rafael: Who takes part in evaluation today? Do you have a review committee with different profiles?

Marina: Yes. We have an internal team and external reviewers. Each project gets scores on criteria such as cultural impact, financial feasibility, audience reach, and social inclusion.

Rafael: Do these criteria change by call for proposals?

Marina: They change a bit. Some criteria are fixed, others vary depending on the event type.

Camila: Understood. Then it is important for the system to allow configurable criteria and weights per call.

Marina: Exactly.

Rafael: Regarding the call lifecycle, which stages do you currently have?

Marina: Publication, submission period, document screening, technical evaluation, ranking, approval, and result publication.

Camila: Do you need an audit trail? For example, tracking who changed status, score, or documents.

Marina: Yes, because we have accountability requirements and formal challenges.

Rafael: And communication with applicants? Do you want automatic messages by stage?

Marina: That would be great. At least submission confirmation, document pending notices, and final result.

Camila: In terms of access, will we have separate roles? Applicant, evaluator, manager, and administrator?

Marina: Yes, these four roles solve our initial needs.

Rafael: Is there a need for appeal/reconsideration after results?

Marina: Yes, but that can be part of phase two, after the core scope.

Camila: About integrations, do you have any finance or institutional protocol systems that must connect to the platform?

Marina: Not in the short term. We only need data export to spreadsheet and PDF.

Rafael: What is the biggest current pain you want to eliminate first?

Marina: Lack of traceability and delays in evaluation. Sometimes we miss deadlines because data is scattered.

Camila: In terms of volume, how many submissions per call do you estimate?

Marina: Between 200 and 1,000, depending on the category.

Rafael: And in terms of timeline, what is your expectation for having something operational?

Marina: A basic version in 4 months, because we have a major call in the second half of the year.

Camila: Great. At a high level, we can plan a first version focused on: call setup, submissions, required documents, evaluation workflow with configurable criteria, and a tracking dashboard.

Marina: That would be excellent to start.

Rafael: Agreed. We will consolidate these initial needs and structure a high-level backlog prioritized by value and risk.

Marina: Perfect.
